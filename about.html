About Sabrina Carpenter

Background

Sabrina Annlynn Carpenter was born on May 11, 1999, in Quakertown, Pennsylvania, and is now an American singer and actress.

She first gained recognition by starring in the Disney Channel series "Girl Meets World" and signing with Hollywood Records. She released her debut single, "Can't Blame a Girl for Trying," in 2014, followed by four studio albums: "Eyes Wide Open" (2015), "Evolution" (2016), "Singular: Act I" (2018), and "Singular: Act II" (2019); three of her singlesâ€”"Alien," "Almost Love," and "Sue Me."

Carpenter moved to Island Records in 2021 and released the single "Skin", which became her first entry on the US Billboard Hot 100. Her fifth album, "Emails I Can't Send" (2022), followed. She opened for Taylor Swift at the Eras Tour in 2023 and released her sixth album "Short n' Sweet" (2024), which debuted at the top of the US Billboard 200 chart, with the Billboard Global 200 number-one singles "Espresso" and "Please Please Please."

Other than Disney, she has acted in several movies, such as "The Hate U Give" (2018) and "Emergency" (2022). She has also starred in Netflix productions like "Tall Girl" (2019), "Tall Girl 2" (2022), and "Work It" (2020). She also played a lead role on Broadway in the musical "Mean Girls" (2020).

Sabrina has performed at major events such as the Jingle Ball, Governors Ball, New Year's Rockin' Eve, Coachella, and more. She also went on two tours with her albums: the "Emails I Can't Send" tour and the "Short N' Sweet" tour.
